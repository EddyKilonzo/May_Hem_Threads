MayHem projects

Mayhem Threads' impactful projects—Borderline, Threads of Dreams, and Climate Justice is Refugee Justice—empower refugees and displaced communities through advocacy, sustainable fashion, and climate action. These initiatives amplify refugee voices, improve living conditions, and address global challenges like climate change and human rights. By fostering resilience and dignity, Mayhem Threads transforms lives while advocating for a more equitable and sustainable future..

MayHem projects

Borderline project

In the arid landscapes along the Kenya-Somalia border, where hope often feels as scarce as water, Borderline, a project by Mayhem Threads, is empowering asylum seekers, migrants, and refugees to reclaim their voices and advocate for their rights. This groundbreaking project equips individuals in refugee camps with the skills and tools to become advocates, ensuring that their stories and struggles are heard in local and global human rights campaigns

Threads of Dreams

This project is empowering refugees to transform discarded materials into beautiful, sustainable fashion while addressing the urgent challenges of climate change. This innovative project is not just about creating clothing—it’s about weaving hope, sustainability, and empowerment into the fabric of displaced communities.


The project has also reduced waste in camps, with tons of discarded materials being repurposed into fashion items. At the same time, refugees are earning income from selling their creations, improving their quality of life and fostering a sense of dignity and pride

Climate Justice is Refugee Justice

This is a transformative project that addresses the interconnected struggles of refugee marginalization, climate change, and human rights. Recognizing that refugees are among the most vulnerable to the impacts of climate change, this initiative seeks to amplify their voices, advocate for their rights, and create sustainable solutions that leave no one behind..


The project demonstrates that the fight for a sustainable planet cannot be separated from the fight for human rights. By addressing the intersectionality of these issues, the project creates a blueprint for inclusive, equitable, and transformative action.
